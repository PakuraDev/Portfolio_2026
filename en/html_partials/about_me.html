The human behind the keyboard

I'm Alejandro, I'm from Seville, I'm 20 years old, I have a beautiful dog and an unhealthy obsession with solving integrals.


Jokes aside, as you may have read, I'm a "design enthusiast." I know it sounds like the biggest cliché in the world, but you'll have to take my word for it, because I basically spend 40% of my time working in Figma, another 40% thinking about what to design, and the remaining 20% trying to center a div.


Outside the screen, I'm just a regular person who enjoys video games, anime, tinkering with new technologies (I'm a Linux user, after all), and spending time with my friends or my dog. As long as nobody forces me to read Sartre, everything will be fine — that's my Sisyphus' rock.

My design philosophy

[Disclaimer: Photo of my dog because it's ART]


I have a particular taste — my favorite interfaces are TUIs (text-based), brutalist, or on the other extreme, futuristic, cyberpunk, or feature-packed designs.


But my priority when designing is not the UI, but the UX. I personally believe a person uses a product because of how easy it is to use, not because of its interface, and the best example is MyFitnessPal. Well, MFP doesn't have great UX or UI, but you get my point.


I could define my philosophy in one sentence: "If my nearly 60-year-old mother, who struggles with Facebook, can use it — or learn to use it in minutes — then anyone can." I know, it's as deep as a kiddie pool, but what matters is that it works.


That said, leaving UX aside, to me good UI is UI made with soul. An interface where you can tell something about the designer who made it. In mine, you can tell I like Linux, or that I love the beach and art.
